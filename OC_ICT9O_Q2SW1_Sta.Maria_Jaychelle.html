Cat Breeds

Image Breed Description
British Shorthair With a characteristically stocky build, dense coat, and large face, the British Shorthair is the pedigreed variation of the common domestic cat seen in Britain. The "British Blue," which has an all-grey-blue coat, orange eyes, and a medium-sized tail, is the most well-known color variation.
Ragdoll A breed of cat known as the Ragdoll has blue eyes and a distinctive colorpoint coat. It has a semi-long, silky-soft coat, and a huge, heavy morphology. In the 1960s, American breeder Ann Baker created the Ragdoll breed. They are most renowned for their friendly attitude and gentle temperament.
Sphynx The Sphynx cat breed, commonly referred to as the Canadian Sphynx, is distinguished by its lack of fur. The Sphynx was created via selective breeding of cats, starting in the 1960s, who naturally have a genetic abnormality that causes them to lack hair.
